Banga Soup with Starch

Origin

Banga soup with starch originated from the Niger-Delta region of Nigeria. It is a delicious soup made from palm nut fruit, an assortment of spices, and a variety of meat and fish. It is one of the most enjoyed dishes by the Urhobos and Itsekiris.

Banga soup is made by extracting the juice from the seeds of raw palm kernel fruit. The seeds are cooked till they are tender and then pounded thoroughly until the juice is extracted and cooked. Starch is very soft and sticky. As such, there is a technique for eating it, by using your index finger and your thumb to press down on one side of the starch, one can cut the size one wants to swallow.

Ingredients

Number Ingredients Measurements Essential values
1 Canned palm nut paste or palm kernel fruit 1 can or 2 derica cups (fresh) star ingredient of the soup
2 Fresh catfish 1 kg can be used with or without (prefarbly used)
3 Protein 1 kg any assortment of protein of one's choosing like tripe, cow leg, goat meat, chicken, turkey, etc
4 Dried seafood ½ kg crayfish, stockfish, dried fish
5 Banga spices ½ kg mixture blend of Ataiko and Irugeje in a dry mill
6 Onion 1 bulb for aromatic feel that boosts the flavor of the cooked protein
7 Season 4 cubes for taste
8 Salt 4 tablespoons for taste
9 Habanero or scotch bonnet or cameroon pepper 4 small pieces used to add a bit of heat to the soup
10 Banga spice leaves (Beletete leaves) 2 tablespoons can substituted with dried basil, bitter leaf or dried scent leaves
11 Liquorice (oburunbebe stick aka banga stick) 1 small stick for that banga soup taste taste
12 Starch ½ cup preferred swallow of choice
13 Palmoil 1 tablespoon to color the edible starch (swallow)
14 Stock/water as desired the palm nut concentrate is quite thick, stock (from boiled protein)/water is needed to loosen it up a bit

Preparation method (canned palm nut)

    BANGA SOUP

  1. Boil the protein with onion, salt, season until it becomes soft
  2. Remove the protein and leave the stock, add the palm nut paste to boil for 30 mins
  3. Add pepper, dried seafood and allow to boil for 5 mins
  4. Add the already cooked protein, fresh catfish, liquorice and banga spices (powder), allow to simmer for 10 mins (to allow the catfish cook)
  5. Add the banga spices (leaves), taste for salt, season and others and cook for 5 mins or until paste is no longer watery

    6. EDIBLE STARCH

  6. In a clean pot, add the edible starch, mix with water to form a not too watery consistency
  7. Add palm oil into the mix
  8. Place pot on fire (low heat) and stir continuously until it thickens and forms a dough with bright yellow color (cooked)
  9. Serve the desired portion in a plate with the banga soup and enjoy.
